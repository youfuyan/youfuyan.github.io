# Content strategy

## What to lead with

1. Production ownership
2. AI/ML systems with rigorous evaluation
3. Reliability and distributed-system judgment
4. Measurable impact
5. Research credibility

## What not to lead with

- a long technology icon wall
- school projects
- generic enthusiasm
- every GitHub repository
- proficiency percentages
- internal Amazon acronyms
- visually loud animations
- a chronological resume dump

## Home-page proof points

Use a small impact strip with accurate qualifiers:

- `~1M/month` uncached image requests evaluated for an AWS-native migration
- `~$96K/year` projected run-rate reduction using public cloud pricing
- `1% to 100%` staged regional production traffic shift with no customer-facing outage
- `IEEE VIS 2024` Honorable Mention

Do not imply every program metric was solely produced by Youfu.
Label projected metrics as projected.

## Case-study selection

### 1. AI image-matting migration

Best evidence for:
- AI/ML systems
- evaluation design
- AWS infrastructure
- cost impact
- cross-team ownership

### 2. Multi-region service launch

Best evidence for:
- operational judgment
- rollout discipline
- incident response
- production reliability
- cross-team debugging

### 3. Event-driven notification service

Best evidence for:
- event-driven correctness
- out-of-order event handling
- DynamoDB conditional writes
- retry and dead-letter handling
- multi-team adoption

### 4. KNOWNet

Best evidence for:
- research depth
- RAG
- knowledge graphs
- human-centered AI
- publication credibility

## Secondary experience

Use an experience timeline rather than full case studies for:

- third-party content ingestion
- external video ingestion
- content-platform migration
- scheduling and rescheduling workflows
- creator tooling and test infrastructure

## Confidentiality

The public site should explain the engineering problem and the result without
publishing internal diagrams, code, operational procedures, customer identifiers,
or confidential architecture details.
